I will start by researching the VTOL design, gathering information about its dimensions, features, and reference images. Then, I will choose a CAD software that I am familiar with or one that meets my needs to work on the model. I will create a basic 2D sketch, incorporating the top, front, and side views of the VTOL as a starting point. From there, I will extrude and shape the surfaces to create a 3D representation, such as the fuselage and wings. Adding all the intricate details like propellers, landing gears, and control surfaces will be my next step. I will apply the right constraints to ensure accurate dimensions and proportions. For moving parts like propellers or rotors, I will create separate components and then assemble them to see how everything fits together and interacts. During the process, I will perform checks to identify and fix any errors or inconsistencies in the design. Additionally, I will apply materials and textures to enhance the visual representation of the VTOL. If possible, I will run simulations to evaluate the VTOL's performance and make any necessary adjustments for optimization. Throughout the entire process, I will continuously review and refine the model until I am satisfied with the final result.
